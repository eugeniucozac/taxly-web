# Taxly — Waitlist Email Drip (pre-launch)

*Created: 9 July 2026 · Sender: Resend · Audience: waitlist signups (EN + ES
variants — write ES natively, don't machine-translate).*

Taxly is pre-launch, so this is a **waitlist nurture** drip, not a product
onboarding. Rules: every email useful on its own, one idea each, no fake
urgency, unsubscribe honored instantly. The drip earns trust before we ever
ask for an SSN — it *is* the product demo.

## Email 1 — instantly: "You're on the list (here's what that means)"

- Confirm the spot; say plainly what Taxly is and isn't yet: *"We open for
  TY2026 returns in January 2027. We are not filing returns yet."*
- Set expectations: ~1 email/month until the season nears, all useful.
- One link: the free tools page — "meanwhile, these work today, no signup."

## Email 2 — day 4: "The price you see is the price you pay (the whole pitch)"

- The honest-pricing story in 150 words: FTC ruling context, the checkout
  ambush, our price-lock. No competitor bashing — link the /vs pages that
  name where rivals win.
- CTA: read Taxly vs TurboTax / vs FreeTaxUSA / vs Direct File.

## Email 3 — day 10: "The tax nobody warns freelancers about"

- The 15.3% SE-tax explainer in plain English + the safe-harbor rule.
- CTA: quarterly tax calculator. (Beachhead relevance filter: this email's
  engagement tells us who the 1099 audience is.)

## Email 4 — seasonal trigger (early January): "Filing season opens — your options, honestly"

- The honest maths: Direct File free if you qualify (say it first),
  FreeTaxUSA cheap if you know your taxes, Taxly if you want guidance.
- Announce actual open date + founding-filer detail (early access order).

## Email 5 — launch day: "We're open — and here's the price lock in writing"

- Tier prices + the price-lock guarantee, stated as a commitment.
- One-click start; reminder the free tier exists: *"If your return is simple,
  you genuinely don't need to pay us."*

## Event triggers (beyond the drip)

- **Tool completion + waitlist join in same session** → tag as high-intent;
  seasonal emails reference the tool they used.
- **ES-locale signups** → ES drip variant, ES community invites.
- **Post-launch, post-first-file** → the **quote ask**: "one sentence about
  the experience we may publish with your first name" — the social-proof
  pipeline (only real, attributed quotes ever appear on the site).
- **T-30 before April 15** (to non-filers on list): deadline reminder that is
  useful even if they file elsewhere — penalty math + extension how-to.

## Do not

- No countdown timers, no "spots running out" (waitlists don't run out).
- No implying IRS authorization before it exists.
- No more than one email in any 7-day window outside January–April.
