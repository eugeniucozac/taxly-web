## The ten recurring questions

**1. "I missed the tax deadline, what do I do?"**
File immediately even if you can't pay — not-filing costs 5%/month vs 0.5%
for not-paying. Pay what you can, set up an IRS payment plan online (halves
the late-pay rate), and if it's your first slip ask for first-time abatement.
Link: penalty estimator + missed-deadline guide.

**2. "First year freelancing — how do taxes even work?"**
Nobody withholds; you pay quarterly. Safe harbor = 100% of last year's tax
(110% over $150k AGI) split in four. Budget ~25–30% of profit; SE tax alone
is 15.3% on 92.35% of profit. Link: quarterly calculator.

**3. "Is TurboTax free actually free?"**
For a narrow set of simple returns, yes; the FTC ruled the advertising
deceptive in 2024 because most people got upsold. Genuinely free routes:
IRS Direct File (if your state participates) and IRS Free File under the AGI
cap. Link: honest alternatives guide.

**4. "Should I use IRS Direct File?"**
If W-2-only + standard deduction + participating state: yes, use it — it's
free and official. It can't do 1099/self-employment income or itemizing.
Link: Direct File guide.

**5. "I got a 1099-K from PayPal/Etsy — am I in trouble?"**
No. It reports gross processed payments, not profit or tax owed. Report your
real net income on Schedule C; keep the form to reconcile. Don't ignore it —
the IRS has a copy. Link: 1099-K guide.

**6. "What's the cheapest way to file with a 1099?"**
Honest answer: FreeTaxUSA at $0 federal + ~$15 state, if you're comfortable
with a form-like interface and know your deductions. Paying more (us
included) buys guidance, not more forms. Link: /vs/freetaxusa.

**7. "Quarterly payment deadline is next week and I haven't saved anything."**
Pay what you can by the date (penalties accrue per-day on the shortfall,
they're not cliff-edge), then fix the runway: safe-harbor number ÷ remaining
quarters. Link: quarterly calculator.

**8. "Do I need to file? I only made $X."**
Below the filing threshold you may not have to — but file anyway if you had
any withholding (refund) or qualify for EITC (refundable, ~1 in 5 eligible
workers miss it). Link: refund estimator.

**9. "Moving to Texas/Florida — does that really save on taxes?"**
On wages, yes — no state return at all. But sales/property taxes are higher,
part-year rules apply in the year you move, and out-of-state income can still
need a nonresident return. Link: no-tax-states guide.

**10. "¿Puedo declarar mis impuestos en español?"**
El IRS ofrece formularios y ayuda en español; la mayoría del software es solo
inglés o traducción parcial. (Honesto: nuestro sitio está en español hoy; el
producto en español llega después del lanzamiento.) Link: glossary ES +
herramientas.
